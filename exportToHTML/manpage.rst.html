6    Where in the world is...?

15   
16   SYNOPSYS 
17   ======== 
18   
19   world [options] [tld, [tld, ...]]
20   
21   
22   DESCRIPTION 
23   =========== 
24   
25   This script takes a list of Internet top-level domain names and prints out
26   where in the world those domains originate from.  Reverse look ups are also
27   supported.
28   
29   
30   EXAMPLES 
31   ======== 
32   
33   Look up top-level domains:: 
34    
35       $ world tz us 
36       tz originates from Tanzania, United Republic of 
37       us originates from United States of America (the) 
38    
39   Reverse lookups:: 
40    
41       $ world -r united 
42       Matches for "united": 
43         ae: United Arab Emirates (the) 
44         gb: United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (the) 
45         tz: Tanzania, United Republic of 
46         uk: United Kingdom (common practice) 
47         um: United States Minor Outlying Islands (the) 
48         us: United States of America (the) 
49    
50   Only two-letter country codes are supported, since these are the only ones
51   that were freely available from the ISO 3166 standard.  As of 2015-01-09, even
52   these are no longer available in machine readable form.
53   
54   This script also knows about non-geographic, generic, USA-centric, historical,
55   common usage, and reserved top-level domains.
56   
57   
58   OPTIONS 
59   ======= 
60   
61   Querying 
62   -------- 
63   
64     -r, --reverse    Do a reverse lookup. In this mode, the arguments can be
65                      any Python regular expression; these are matched against
66                      all TLD descriptions (e.g. country names) and a list of
67                      matches is printed.
68     -a, --all        Print the mapping of all top-level domains.
69   
70   
71   Other 
72   ----- 
73   
74     -h, --help       show this help message and exit
75     --version        show program's version number and exit
76   
77   
78   With no top-level domains given, help is printed.
